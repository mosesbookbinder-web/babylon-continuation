# Observer-Admissible Selection Principle

Admissibility is not determined by reducing artifacts to a single truth.

A full artifact field may be arbitrarily large and structurally valid.

However, an observer operating under bounded perception cannot directly evaluate the full field.

A selection must be applied.

This selection must:

- preserve structural invariants
- maintain linkage integrity
- expose constraint boundaries
- avoid collapsing uncertainty into false completion

The selected subset is not "the truth".

It is the minimal structure required for an observer to recognize admissibility.

Over-selection introduces distortion.
Under-selection obscures structure.

Admissibility is therefore recognized through invariant-preserving selection under observer constraints.
